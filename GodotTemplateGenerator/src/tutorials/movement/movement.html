Character Movement in Godot

An overview of different movement systems and when to use them

Introduction to Character Movement

Character movement is one of the fundamental aspects of game development. How your character moves affects the feel of your game and is often the primary way players interact with your world. In Godot, there are several approaches to implementing character movement, each suited to different game types.

This guide provides an overview of different movement systems available in Godot and helps you choose the right one for your project.

Types of Movement Systems

2D Platform Movement

Used in side-scrolling platformers like Super Mario, Hollow Knight, or Celeste. Characterized by:

  • Horizontal movement along a 2D plane
  • Jumping and gravity mechanics
  • Often involves collision with platforms and obstacles

Learn more about 2D Platform Movement →

Top-down Movement

Used in games with a bird's-eye view like The Legend of Zelda, Hotline Miami, or Enter the Gungeon. Features:

  • Movement in four or eight directions
  • Usually no gravity involved
  • Often includes collision with walls and objects

Learn more about Top-down Movement →

First-person Movement

Used in FPS games, walking simulators, and VR experiences. Characteristics include:

  • Camera control for looking around
  • WASD-style movement relative to camera direction
  • Often includes features like jumping, crouching, and sprinting

Learn more about First-person Movement →

Point-and-click Movement

Used in adventure games, RTS games, and some RPGs. Key features:

  • Character moves to where the player clicks
  • Often involves pathfinding around obstacles
  • May include interaction with objects upon arrival

Learn more about Point-and-click Movement →

Choosing the Right Movement System

When selecting a movement system for your game, consider:

Game Perspective

The camera perspective (side view, top-down, first-person) will greatly influence your movement system choice.

Game Feel

Different movement systems create different feelings - platformers often feel bouncy and precise, while top-down games can feel fluid and responsive.

Control Scheme

Consider your target platform and what input methods (keyboard/mouse, controller, touch) will be primary for your game.
